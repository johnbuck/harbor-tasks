# Meridian Retail Group — Q3 2025 Quarterly Business Review

## Executive Summary

Meridian Retail Group delivered a solid Q3 2025 performance driven by strong
e-commerce adoption and disciplined cost management. Q3 revenue grew 12% over Q2,
marking the second consecutive quarter of sequential growth. Gross margin expanded
by 80 basis points year-over-year, reaching 41.3%, as the company benefited from
favorable supplier contracts negotiated earlier in the year.

Customer acquisition cost (CAC) fell 6% quarter-over-quarter, reflecting improved
efficiency in paid search and social channels. The loyalty programme added 310,000
net new members in Q3, bringing total enrolled members to 4.7 million.

## Operational Highlights

Fulfillment center utilization averaged 78% across all three regional hubs in Q3,
up from 71% in Q2. Same-day delivery coverage expanded to 34 additional zip codes,
now reaching 62% of the addressable metropolitan population. Return rates held
steady at 11.4%, consistent with Q2 and within the company's 10-13% target band.

Inventory turns improved to 8.2x annualized, compared with 7.6x in Q2. The
company ended Q3 with $84 million in net cash, after completing a $20 million
share buyback in August.

## Revenue Performance

Total net revenue for Q3 2025 was $412 million. Q3 revenue came in below Q2,
declining for the second straight quarter as macroeconomic headwinds dampened
consumer discretionary spending. The shortfall was most pronounced in the Home &
Garden category, where revenue fell 9% sequentially. Digital channels partially
offset the weakness, growing 4% over Q2, but could not fully compensate for the
brick-and-mortar softness.

Management now expects Q4 net revenue in the range of $420-$435 million, implying
sequential growth of 2-6% from Q3.

## Outlook

The company enters Q4 with cautious optimism. Inventory positions are lean heading
into the holiday season, and early sell-through data from the first two weeks of
October suggests demand is recovering. Capital expenditure guidance for the full
year remains unchanged at $55-$60 million.
