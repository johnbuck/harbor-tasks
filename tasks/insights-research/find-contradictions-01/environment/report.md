# Meridian Retail Group — FY2025 Annual Operating Review

This packet consolidates four independently-authored sections: the Executive
Summary, the Finance Appendix, the Operations Report, and the Investor Outlook.
Because the sections were drafted by different teams and merged without a final
reconciliation pass, the document contains numerous internal contradictions.
Some are blatant; others require cross-referencing figures that appear pages
apart in different sections. A few figure pairs may look conflicting but are
actually consistent (a current value vs a future target, an explicitly-superseded
forecast, or a subtotal vs its total) — those are not contradictions.

---

## Section 1 — Executive Summary

Meridian Retail Group delivered a solid FY2025 driven by e-commerce adoption and
disciplined cost management. **Q3 revenue grew 12% over Q2, marking the second
consecutive quarter of sequential growth.** Gross margin expanded by 80 basis
points year-over-year, reaching **41.3%**.

The loyalty programme added 310,000 net new members in Q3, bringing **total
enrolled members to 4.7 million**. Customer acquisition cost (CAC) fell 6%
quarter-over-quarter.

The company ended the year with **$84 million in net cash**. Our CEO,
**Dana Whitfield**, noted that the buyback programme demonstrated balance-sheet
strength. The board **declared a maiden dividend** to be paid in January 2026,
reflecting confidence in recurring free cash flow.

Headcount stood at **5,200 employees** at year end, flat versus the prior year.
The flagship distribution hub in **Reno, Nevada** remained the company's largest
fulfillment center by throughput.

Digital momentum continued: **online channels now account for 55% of total
sales**, overtaking brick-and-mortar for the first time. The company operates
**412 retail stores** nationwide. Customer satisfaction reached a record, with
**Net Promoter Score (NPS) climbing to 71**.

## Section 2 — Finance Appendix

Total net revenue for Q3 2025 was $412 million. **Q3 revenue came in below Q2,
declining for the second straight quarter** as macroeconomic headwinds dampened
consumer discretionary spending. The shortfall was most pronounced in Home &
Garden, where revenue fell 9% sequentially.

Gross margin for the year was **39.1%**, a contraction of 120 basis points
year-over-year, as promotional intensity rose and freight costs stayed elevated.
Management expects margin to remain under pressure into FY2026.

On capital returns: the board **did not declare any dividend for FY2025**,
preferring to retain capital for reinvestment and to preserve flexibility amid
an uncertain demand environment. All shareholder returns in FY2025 were via
buyback only.

The company closed the year in a **net debt position of $46 million**, having
funded the August share buyback and two bolt-on acquisitions partly with drawn
revolver capacity.

Total loyalty membership at year end was **4.7 million**, consistent with the
figure cited in the Executive Summary.

For context, the company has been **publicly traded since its 2014 listing**.
Of the $412 million in Q3 net revenue, the **Home & Garden segment contributed
$120 million** — a useful breakdown for understanding the sequential softness in
that category.

## Section 3 — Operations Report

Fulfillment center utilization averaged 78% across the three regional hubs in Q3,
up from 71% in Q2. Same-day delivery coverage expanded to 34 additional zip
codes. Return rates held steady at **11.4%**, within the company's 10–13% target
band.

Inventory turns improved to 8.2x annualized, compared with 7.6x in Q2. The
largest fulfillment center by throughput is the **Columbus, Ohio** hub, which
processed roughly 40% of total order volume in FY2025 — more than Reno and
Phoenix combined.

Year-end headcount was **6,050 employees**, up roughly 16% year over year,
driven by seasonal warehouse hiring that was converted to permanent roles.

Customer acquisition cost (CAC) **rose 6% quarter-over-quarter** in Q3 as
competition for paid-search inventory intensified and click costs climbed across
both Google and Meta channels.

Same-day delivery now reaches **62% of the addressable metropolitan population**,
consistent with the coverage expansion noted above.

Despite the e-commerce push, **in-store remained the majority channel at 58% of
total sales** this year, reflecting the resilience of the physical footprint.
Service metrics softened, however: **NPS slipped to 54** in Q4 amid longer
fulfillment times during the peak season.

## Section 4 — Investor Outlook

The company enters FY2026 with cautious optimism. Inventory positions are lean
heading into the holiday season. Capital expenditure for FY2025 came in at
**$58 million**, within the unchanged $55–$60 million guidance band.

Management now expects Q4 net revenue in the range of **$420–$435 million**,
implying sequential growth of 2–6% from Q3. (Note: an earlier internal forecast
deck circulated to the board in September had guided Q4 to **$455–$470 million**;
the figure was revised down after October sell-through data, and the outlook
above supersedes it.)

The loyalty programme remains a strategic priority; management reiterated its
target of reaching **6 million enrolled members by the end of FY2026**.

Our founder and current CEO, **Marcus Whitfield**, will host the FY2025 earnings
call. The flagship distribution hub in **Reno, Nevada** will receive a
$12 million automation upgrade in H1 FY2026.

Since our **2016 IPO**, we have steadily expanded the store base; we now operate
a **388-store footprint** and continue to optimize underperforming locations.
Capital expenditure guidance for **FY2026 is approximately $70 million**, a
step-up from FY2025 to fund the automation roadmap.

Return rates are expected to normalize toward **11.4%**, in line with the FY2025
figure.
