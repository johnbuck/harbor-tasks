A printing house begins the day with forty-two bound volumes. The printing house is staffed by thirty-one people that day. First, 5 bound volumes are written off after a spill. Next, eleven bound volumes are pulled for inspection and set aside. After that, the crew restocks 9 additional bound volumes. Later, the running total is tripled by an overnight consolidation. Soon after, three bound volumes are shipped out and removed from the count. By midday, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN fifty, 5 bound volumes are removed; OTHERWISE 3 more bound volumes are added. In the afternoon, a late shipment adds 11 bound volumes. Before closing, an order ships, taking 9 bound volumes away. Finally, look up the calibration offset published for reference code CAL-08 in the site registry and add that offset to the running total. How many bound volumes remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
