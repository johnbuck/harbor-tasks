A seed bank begins the day with fifty-seven labelled samples. First, a late shipment adds ten labelled samples. A nearby sign notes the seed bank was founded eighty-three years earlier. Next, eight labelled samples arrive from a partner site. After that, 13 labelled samples are written off after a spill. For reference, the seed bank keeps sixty-eight aisles, though that does not change any count. Later, a delivery brings three more labelled samples. Soon after, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN 35, 11 labelled samples are removed; OTHERWISE 3 more labelled samples are added. By midday, an order ships, taking three labelled samples away. In the afternoon, 11 labelled samples are shipped out and removed from the count. Before closing, a late shipment adds three labelled samples. How many labelled samples remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
