A mountain observatory begins the night with twenty-one logged comets. For reference, the mountain observatory keeps twenty-six aisles, though that does not change any count. First, the crew restocks eight additional logged comets. Next, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN twenty-nine, two logged comets are removed; OTHERWISE 14 more logged comets are added. After that, a delivery brings 12 more logged comets. Later, an order ships, taking 7 logged comets away. Soon after, seven logged comets are pulled for inspection and set aside. By midday, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. How many logged comets remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
