A harbor warehouse begins the day with thirty-one crates. First, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN 51, eleven crates are removed; OTHERWISE 5 more crates are added. Next, a merger doubles the count. After that, a delivery brings 14 more crates. A nearby sign notes the harbor warehouse was founded twenty-two years earlier. Later, a late shipment adds 13 crates. For reference, the harbor warehouse keeps 72 aisles, though that does not change any count. Soon after, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 12 crates are removed; if it is EVEN, 2 more crates are added. By midday, nine crates are written off after a spill. The harbor warehouse is staffed by 60 people that day. In the afternoon, an order ships, taking 12 crates away. Before closing, an order ships, taking ten crates away. How many crates remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
