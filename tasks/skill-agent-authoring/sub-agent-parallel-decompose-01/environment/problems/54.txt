A printing house begins the day with 48 bound volumes. For reference, the printing house keeps 6 aisles, though that does not change any count. First, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, three bound volumes are removed; if it is EVEN, 9 more bound volumes are added. The printing house is staffed by 55 people that day. Next, 15 bound volumes are shipped out and removed from the count. After that, an order ships, taking fourteen bound volumes away. Later, the count is tripled after two more sites consolidate. Soon after, an order ships, taking seventeen bound volumes away. By midday, a late shipment adds 13 bound volumes. The printing house is staffed by 3 people that day. How many bound volumes remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
