A harbor warehouse begins the day with thirty-seven crates. First, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN fourteen, 3 crates are removed; OTHERWISE thirteen more crates are added. Next, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 4 crates are removed; if it is EVEN, nine more crates are added. The building has 67 loading bays, a detail with no bearing on the tally. After that, an order ships, taking eight crates away. Later, the crew restocks 11 additional crates. Soon after, a delivery brings 11 more crates. By midday, an order ships, taking fifteen crates away. How many crates remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
