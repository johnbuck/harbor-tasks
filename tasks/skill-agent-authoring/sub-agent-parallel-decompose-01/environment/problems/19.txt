A recycling center begins the shift with 60 sorted bins. Background: seventy-eight visitors toured the recycling center, unrelated to the inventory. First, 7 sorted bins are pulled for inspection and set aside. Next, a delivery brings thirteen more sorted bins. After that, 18 sorted bins are shipped out and removed from the count. Later, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. Soon after, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 7 more sorted bins are added; if it is EVEN, exactly half of them are removed. By midday, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN 46, thirteen sorted bins are removed; OTHERWISE eleven more sorted bins are added. In the afternoon, 7 sorted bins are written off after a spill. The recycling center is staffed by 2 people that shift. Before closing, 15 sorted bins are pulled for inspection and set aside. How many sorted bins remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
