A printing house begins the day with thirty-eight bound volumes. First, a late shipment adds fifteen bound volumes. Next, 10 bound volumes are pulled for inspection and set aside. The printing house is staffed by seventy people that day. After that, fourteen bound volumes are pulled for inspection and set aside. Later, a late shipment adds thirteen bound volumes. Soon after, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN 32, eight bound volumes are removed; OTHERWISE two more bound volumes are added. By midday, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. Finally, look up the calibration offset published for reference code CAL-24 in the site registry and add that offset to the running total. How many bound volumes remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
