A seed bank begins the day with thirty-six labelled samples. First, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 3 more labelled samples are added; if it is EVEN, exactly half of them are removed. Next, seven labelled samples are shipped out and removed from the count. After that, five labelled samples arrive from a partner site. The building has 12 loading bays, a detail with no bearing on the tally. Later, five labelled samples are pulled for inspection and set aside. Soon after, the running total is tripled by an overnight consolidation. By midday, a late shipment adds 9 labelled samples. How many labelled samples remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
