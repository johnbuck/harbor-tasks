A harbor warehouse begins the day with 38 crates. First, the crew restocks 10 additional crates. Next, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. After that, 3 crates are shipped out and removed from the count. Later, 6 crates are pulled for inspection and set aside. The harbor warehouse is staffed by eighty-seven people that day. Soon after, thirteen crates are pulled for inspection and set aside. By midday, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 3 more crates are added; if it is EVEN, exactly half of them are removed. In the afternoon, a delivery brings 6 more crates. Before closing, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 10 crates are removed; if it is EVEN, one more crates are added. How many crates remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
