A bakery cooperative begins the morning with forty-two sourdough loaves. First, 17 sourdough loaves are written off after a spill. Next, a merger doubles the count. After that, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, eleven more sourdough loaves are added; if it is EVEN, exactly half of them are removed. Later, 9 sourdough loaves arrive from a partner site. Soon after, a delivery brings fourteen more sourdough loaves. The building has 38 loading bays, a detail with no bearing on the tally. By midday, a late shipment adds eight sourdough loaves. How many sourdough loaves remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
