A harbor warehouse begins the day with 35 crates. Mid-morning, a delivery brings six more crates. At noon the count is tripled after two more shipments arrive. In the afternoon, 10 crates are sent out and removed from the count. At closing, because the running total is an odd number, the supervisor adds 3 more crates. Overnight, 10 crates are found damaged and discarded.

How many crates remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
