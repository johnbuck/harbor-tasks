A printing house begins the day with fifty-two bound volumes. First, seven bound volumes are written off after a spill. Next, a delivery brings eight more bound volumes. After that, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. A nearby sign notes the printing house was founded 88 years earlier. Later, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN 61, 12 bound volumes are removed; OTHERWISE 13 more bound volumes are added. Soon after, a late shipment adds ten bound volumes. By midday, eighteen bound volumes are pulled for inspection and set aside. How many bound volumes remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
