A seed bank begins the day with fifty-one labelled samples. A nearby sign notes the seed bank was founded four years earlier. First, seven labelled samples are shipped out and removed from the count. For reference, the seed bank keeps three aisles, though that does not change any count. Next, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 11 labelled samples are removed; if it is EVEN, eight more labelled samples are added. After that, the count is tripled after two more sites consolidate. Later, thirteen labelled samples are shipped out and removed from the count. Soon after, an order ships, taking 17 labelled samples away. How many labelled samples remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
