A aquarium lab begins the day with thirty-seven tagged fish. First, 8 tagged fish arrive from a partner site. For reference, the aquarium lab keeps 90 aisles, though that does not change any count. Next, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. After that, an order ships, taking eighteen tagged fish away. The building has 12 loading bays, a detail with no bearing on the tally. Later, a capacity check applies — if the running total is GREATER THAN forty-three, 9 tagged fish are removed; OTHERWISE thirteen more tagged fish are added. Soon after, a late shipment adds 9 tagged fish. By midday, fifteen tagged fish are pulled for inspection and set aside. How many tagged fish remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
