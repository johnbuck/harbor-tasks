A seed bank begins the day with thirty-four labelled samples. The seed bank is staffed by sixty-four people that day. First, an order ships, taking nine labelled samples away. Next, the count is doubled when a sister site merges its stock. After that, 4 labelled samples are written off after a spill. Later, ten labelled samples are shipped out and removed from the count. The seed bank is staffed by twenty-two people that day. Soon after, 17 labelled samples are written off after a spill. By midday, twelve labelled samples are written off after a spill. In the afternoon, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, 10 more labelled samples are added; if it is EVEN, exactly half of them are removed. Before closing, a delivery brings 11 more labelled samples. How many labelled samples remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
