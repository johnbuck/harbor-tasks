A bicycle workshop begins the day with 24 repaired frames. A nearby sign notes the bicycle workshop was founded twenty-five years earlier. First, five repaired frames are pulled for inspection and set aside. Next, a late shipment adds 14 repaired frames. The building has 39 loading bays, a detail with no bearing on the tally. After that, a delivery brings 13 more repaired frames. Later, twelve repaired frames are shipped out and removed from the count. Soon after, a merger doubles the count. By midday, a parity rule applies — if the running total is ODD at that point, seven repaired frames are removed; if it is EVEN, five more repaired frames are added. In the afternoon, eight repaired frames are written off after a spill. Before closing, five repaired frames are pulled for inspection and set aside. How many repaired frames remain at the very end? Respond with the single final integer only.
